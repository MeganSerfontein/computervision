About the Artist

Hello, I'm Megan Serfontein

A citizen of many places, only one of which being my physical home in South Africa. I find myself far more drawn to the many digital realms I have become involved in. From diving into various computer games to making multiple different blogs in support of varied interests, I have many roots in the digital world.

It is because of this that I became fascinated by how technology can influence and curate a person's sense of self. Similarly, I am also very aware of the disturbing undertones present in a person’s image and digital self becoming easily-accessed and recorded data, constantly monitored by the devices that are so ingrained in our daily lives.

My Story

Computer Vision

This digital facsination sparked an engagement with the concept of a technologically-driven perception of reality, specifically where the self, and self within space, is concerned. My work uses simple methods of intervention, inspired by modern phenomena such as the selfie, vlogs (video blogs), surveillance, and digital effects, and is brought about through the practice of programming and interactivity.

My work investigates not only the careful, considered action that takes place before a camera, leading to an intended image, but also the implication of being seen and actively looked at by a lens or others. Additionally, my work investigates ideas of mapping the transient networking inherent to spaces (both digital and physical) by utilising programs that abstract what they see. These programs mimic the notion of digital remnants by translating simpler data points such as movement or colour into “graphs”- a rendering of what the device deems to have effected it.

a little snippet more

Beyond the digital veneer, I also have a great love of writing and printmaking, with another body of work still in process that combined these two. My favourite printmaking techniques would be drypoint and monotyping for their versatility and range. My digital practice is heavily influenced by my printmaking one, particularly the more graphical programs that came directly out of a body of work where I drypointed my daily map of my mouse movements whilst working on the computer.

Receiving a Sasol New Signtatures 2018 Merit Award for one of my digital pieces has been a highlight for me, and makes me excited for where my work will grow to next. Perhaps it is time to revisit my printmaking.
